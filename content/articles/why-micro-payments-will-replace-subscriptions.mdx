## The Subscription Problem

The average American pays for 4.5 subscription services they rarely use. That's roughly $50/month on content consumed maybe 20% of the time. The math is brutal: you're paying $600/year for things you value at $120.

The subscription model was a hack built on infrastructure limitations. Credit card fees of $0.30 + 2.9% made anything under $5 economically irrational. So platforms bundled everything into monthly plans and hoped you'd forget to cancel.

## The Alignment Problem

Subscriptions create a fundamental three-way misalignment:

**Readers** try to "get their money's worth" — binge-reading content they don't care about just to justify the subscription.

**Platforms** optimize for retention over quality — keeping people subscribed regardless of whether they're getting value.

**Writers** produce volume over quality — more articles means more page views means more subscription renewals, regardless of depth.

Micro-payments fix all three. Readers only pay for what they actually read. Platform revenue correlates directly with content quality. Writers are incentivized to produce fewer, better articles.

## The Math That Makes It Possible

On Ethereum L1, a single ERC-20 token transfer costs $0.50–$2.00 in gas. At $0.001 per read, the gas cost is **500–2000× the transaction value**. This is economically absurd — it's like paying $5 in bank fees to move a single penny.

Arc changes this fundamentally. USDC-native gas and sub-cent transaction costs make $0.001 payments practical at scale. The overhead is less than 10% of the transaction value — the same ratio as credit card fees on a $10 purchase.

## What This Means for Readers

A moderate reader — 3-5 articles per day — spends $0.003–$0.005 per day, or roughly $0.10–$0.15 per month. Compare that to $15/month for a traditional subscription. That's a **30–150× reduction** in cost for the same content consumption.

And your idle balance earns yield. If you deposit $0.10, most of it auto-stakes into USYC while you browse. By the time you've read 10 articles, your USYC position has earned a fraction of a cent in yield. Small now, but the math compounds.

## What This Means for Writers

A niche writer with 1,000 daily readers earns $1.00/day directly from reads — no platform taking 40–70%, no minimum payout thresholds, no monthly delays. Every read settles in under a second on Arc.

Their earnings auto-compound into USYC. After a month of 30,000 reads, they've earned $30 in principal plus ~$0.004 in yield — and that yield scales with their earnings balance.

## The Future

Micro-payments are the foundation of a per-action economy. Not just reading — every API call, AI generation request, data query, and compute second can be priced at its true marginal cost and settled instantly.

The question was never whether people would pay for content. It was whether the payment infrastructure could handle sub-cent transactions economically. Arc answers that question.
