## What Makes Arc Different

Every EVM chain uses its native token for gas. Ethereum uses ETH. Polygon uses MATIC. BNB Chain uses BNB. This creates a tax: before you can do anything on-chain, you need to acquire the chain's native token.

Arc breaks this pattern. Gas on Arc is paid in USDC. This is not a trivial change — it fundamentally restructures what kinds of applications are economically viable.

## The Gas Cost Comparison

Let's run the numbers for a $0.001 micro-payment:

| Chain | Gas Cost | Overhead |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Ethereum L1 | $0.50–$2.00 | 500–2000× |
| Polygon | $0.001–$0.01 | 1–10× |
| Arc | <$0.0001 | <10% |

At 500–2000× overhead, Ethereum L1 micro-payments are economically impossible. At 10× overhead, Polygon is marginal. At <10%, Arc makes per-read pricing viable at scale.

## USDC as Gas: What This Means for UX

Traditional Web3 UX is hostile to new users. "You need ETH to pay for gas to send USDC" is not a sentence a non-crypto person can parse. On Arc, you just need USDC. The mental model collapses to something users already understand: a dollar is a dollar.

This has compounding effects on application design. When gas fees are denominated in stablecoins, you can:

- Display exact costs to users upfront ("This will cost $0.001")
- Predict transaction costs in your application logic
- Design payment flows that don't require users to maintain gas reserves

## The USYC Teller Integration

The most powerful piece of the Arc ecosystem is the Hashnote USYC Teller. This contract enables T+0 atomic USDC ↔ USYC conversion.

```solidity
// Subscribe USDC → USYC
uint256 usycMinted = teller.subscribe(usdcAmount);

// Redeem USYC → USDC (T+0, atomic)
uint256 usdcOut = teller.redeem(usycAmount);
```

This means your application can hold user funds in yield-bearing USYC automatically and redeem atomically at the moment of purchase. The user never manually stakes or unstakes — it happens transparently.

## What to Build

The combination of USDC gas, micro-payments, and yield-bearing stablecoins opens up an entire class of applications that weren't previously viable:

- **Pay-per-use APIs** at $0.0001 per call
- **Per-minute streaming payments** for video or audio
- **Per-compute AI inference** pricing
- **Micro-tipping** for social media content

Arc is the infrastructure layer that makes the per-action economy possible. Build accordingly.
